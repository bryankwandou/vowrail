# Competitive Landscape

## Direct Category

| Product class | What it does well | Structural gap Vowrail targets |
|---|---|---|
| x402 gateways | Standard payment challenge and retry flow | Payment transport does not define operator intent |
| Agent wallets | Custody, signing, balances, session keys | Permissions are often coarse and vendor-specific |
| Transaction simulators | Decode and preview chain effects | Usually lack reusable business policy and receipts |
| API marketplaces | Provider discovery and billing | Do not govern arbitrary agent-originated transactions |
| Smart accounts | Programmable authority | Need usable policy authoring and decision evidence |

## Wedge

The first customer is a team operating agents that can spend treasury funds. Vowrail wins or loses on integration speed, policy clarity, and evidence quality. It should not compete as another wallet, gateway, or marketplace.

## Defensible Assets

1. A portable mandate schema adopted by agent frameworks.
2. Provider and program risk history derived from real decisions.
3. Auditable policy templates for common autonomous workflows.
4. Cross-rail evidence linking intent, simulation, approval, and settlement.
